## Gaps

### 1. BABILong (k-step reasoning in haystacks) has never been evaluated with TRM-style architectural recursion on a subquadratic backbone, despite being the only benchmark that explicitly varies a "reasoning depth" knob inside a long-context haystack.

**Type:** Domain-transfer gap (D = short-context puzzles; D' = long-context k-step reasoning).
**Statement.** BABILong (2406.10149) parameterizes both haystack length (0–10M tokens) and reasoning depth k≤5, and the BABILong authors explicitly observe that "recurrent memory transformers handle longer contexts than attention-based models at equal parameters." Yet no TRM/HRM/Huginn/Ouro/MoR/LoopFormer paper (2510.04871, 2506.21734, 2502.05171, 2510.25741, 2507.10524, 2602.11451) has reported BABILong numbers — they all benchmark on Sudoku, ARC-AGI, GSM8K, MMLU at <8k tokens. The (architectural-recursion × subquadratic-backbone) cell of the design grid is empty on the only benchmark where the (k, context length) plane is jointly observable in published numbers. A falsifiable axis: held-fixed haystack length L, vary k ∈ {1,2,3,4,5} and the architectural recursion count K ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8}, observe whether accuracy at large (k, L) is monotone in K under SubQ backbone but flat under dense.
**Evidence:** BABILong (2406.10149); recursion architectures: TRM (2510.04871), HRM (2506.21734) + critique (2601.10679), Huginn (2502.05171), Ouro/LoopLM (2510.25741), MoR (2507.10524), LoopFormer (2602.11451), PLT (2510.24824).
**Verification query:** `hf_papers search "looped transformer BABILong reasoning haystack"` returned 10 results — every recursion-architecture hit (LoopFormer 2602.11451, Adaptive Loops 2603.08391, Reasoning with Latent Thoughts 2502.17416, PLT 2510.24824, Ouro 2510.25741, Looped Transformers Learning Algorithms 2311.12424, On Expressive Power 2410.01405, Hyperloop 2604.21254) reports synthetic puzzles or short-context language-model benchmarks; zero report BABILong. `hf_papers search "TRM tiny recursive model long context benchmark"` returned 10 long-context-benchmark papers, none of which evaluate any recursive-loop architecture.
**Why it matters for the spec.** The spec's joint (chain-length × context-length) novelty target requires a benchmark where both axes co-vary. BABILong is the cleanest such instrument in the open literature, and the fusion thesis is uniquely positioned to be tested or falsified there.

### 2. Michelangelo / Latent Structure Queries (LSQ) is the strongest argument that long-context bottleneck is latent-structure inference rather than retrieval — exactly the regime architectural recursion targets — yet no recursion paper engages with it because the eval is closed.

**Type:** Untested assumption (papers in scout-1 cluster all assume "depth-time recursion adds reasoning"; nobody tests it on the eval most explicitly designed to isolate latent-structure inference).
**Statement.** Michelangelo (2409.12640) reports super-linear degradation beyond 32k on graph/code-state/list-ops tasks where retrieval is trivialized and only latent-structure inference matters. Every recursive-transformer paper claims its loop adds reasoning depth (TRM 2510.04871, HRM 2506.21734, Huginn 2502.05171, Ouro 2510.25741, LoopFormer 2602.11451, MoR 2507.10524) but supports the claim with short-context puzzles whose latent structure is small. The proxy for "is recursion adding reasoning?" is therefore methodologically weak on exactly the load-bearing axis. Because Michelangelo is closed, no replication path exists; the gap is "open replication of LSQ-style tasks evaluated against recursion-depth K under a SubQ backbone." Falsifiable axis: open replication of LSQ graph + list-ops sub-tasks, recursion depth K ∈ {1,…,8}, context length L ∈ {8k, 32k, 128k}; the spec's claim succeeds iff the K-dependent accuracy slope flattens less under SubQ + recursion than under dense or under SubQ-without-recursion.
**Evidence:** Michelangelo / LSQ (2409.12640); recursion architectures (2510.04871, 2506.21734, 2502.05171, 2510.25741, 2507.10524, 2602.11451). Ablation precedent: HRM mechanistic critique (2601.10679) showing recursive fixed point is fragile.
**Verification query:** `hf_papers search "recursion depth axis fixed context length benchmark"` returned 10 results — no benchmark explicitly varies architectural recursion depth as an evaluation axis. The closest hit is 100-LongBench (2505.19293) which controls input length but not architectural recursion. Confirms no published instrument couples the two axes.
**Why it matters for the spec.** The spec's "is recursion productive on SubQ?" question reduces to "does adding K passes preserve or amplify latent-structure inference?" Michelangelo-style LSQ is the canonical instrument for that question and is currently a closed-source measurement-shaped hole.

### 3. The Sparse Frontier method-by-task taxonomy has been benchmarked at 32k–256k retrieval but never under chain-length-scaling failure modes — the spec lives exactly where the two axes collide.

**Type:** Unexplored intersection (task-conditional sparse-attention method choice × chain-length-scaling regime).
**Statement.** Sparse Frontier (2504.17768) reports that the optimal sparse-attention pattern is task-conditional: Vertical-Slash for retrieval, Block-Sparse for reasoning, Quest for decode; longer sequences tolerate higher sparsity. None of the chain-length-scaling literature (Inverse Scaling in Test-Time Compute 2507.14417; When More is Less 2502.07266; Effective Reasoning / FSF 2509.19284; Through the Valley 2506.07712; Thinking-Optimal Scaling 2502.18080; L1 / LCPO 2503.04697; InftyThink 2503.06692) studies subquadratic-attention models, so "does longer reasoning chain tolerate higher sparsity" is unmeasured. The fusion thesis is precisely a claim about this intersection: K passes of architectural recursion may amortize sparse-attention's information loss, or compound it via Tunnel-Vision-style failure (2509.04475). Falsifiable axis: hold context length and dense-baseline accuracy fixed; vary sparsity ratio s ∈ {0.1, 0.25, 0.5} × CoT length n ∈ {128, 512, 2048, 8192} on MATH-500/Omni-MATH; predict whether accuracy(s, n) inverted-U shifts under recursion vs. CoT-only.
**Evidence:** Sparse Frontier (2504.17768); chain-length scaling: 2507.14417, 2502.07266, 2509.19284, 2506.07712, 2502.18080, 2503.04697, 2503.06692; failure mode: Tunnel Vision / ParaThinker (2509.04475); Score Dilution at Test Time (2512.13898).
**Verification query:** `hf_papers search "subquadratic attention chain of thought scaling reasoning length"` returned 10 results; not one couples a subquadratic-attention model with the inverse-scaling phenomenon. ThinkEdit (2503.22048) and Thinking-Optimal-Scaling (2502.18080) both target dense reasoning models; Inverse Scaling (2507.14417) explicitly enumerates Claude/o-series/DeepSeek/Qwen — all dense.
**Why it matters for the spec.** The bibliography headline "none of the 9 chain-length-scaling papers studies a subquadratic-attention model" is the clearest open-literature gap; this gap operationalizes it via Sparse Frontier's task-conditional taxonomy as the design knob.

### 4. NoCha and Loong (narrative + cross-document reasoning) cleanly separate retrieval from reasoning over the needle but have never been used to disentangle subquadratic-attention's information loss from its reasoning-depth deficit.

**Type:** Missing baseline (every SubQ-attention evaluation cluster uses NIAH/RULER as primary long-context measure; NoCha/Loong are obviously stronger reasoning-over-the-needle baselines yet are absent from SubQ evaluations).
**Statement.** NoCha (2406.16264) reports best models at ~55% on book-length true/false claim pairs (humans 95%+) — a clean R++ benchmark uncorrelated with NIAH. Loong (2406.17419) demonstrates 20–30 absolute-point drops moving from Spotlight (R) to ChainOfReasoning (R++) at fixed length. Yet NSA (DSA / DeepSeek-V3.2 / 2512.02556 + scout-2 NSA/MoBA cluster) and the SSM/linear-attention papers (M1 2504.10449, Scaling Reasoning without Attention 2505.22425, Mamba-2 hybrid 2406.07887, LongMamba 2504.16053, ReMamba 2408.15496) report only NIAH/RULER/PG-19 perplexity and short-context reasoning. The fusion thesis predicts asymmetric behavior: recursion adds reasoning depth without changing retrieval, sparse attention may impair retrieval but spare reasoning, so the (R, R+, R++) decomposition on NoCha+Loong should exhibit a distinctive signature for the fusion vs each ablation. Falsifiable axis: NoCha pair-accuracy and Loong ChainOfReasoning sub-task scores; predict that SubQ-without-recursion drops on R but matches dense on R++ (under fixed K=1), and that SubQ+recursion shows non-monotone gap by sub-task.
**Evidence:** NoCha (2406.16264); Loong (2406.17419); SubQ-attention NSA cluster (2502.11089 NSA, 2502.13189 MoBA, 2512.02556 DSA, Quest etc.); SSM/linear: M1 (2504.10449), Scaling Reasoning without Attention (2505.22425), LongMamba (2504.16053), ReMamba (2408.15496), Mamba-2 hybrid (2406.07887).
**Verification query:** `hf_papers search "Mamba RULER NIAH long context evaluation hybrid"` returned 10 results; LongMamba, ReMamba, Mamba-2 hybrid all report RULER/NIAH/perplexity, none report NoCha or Loong. `hf_papers search "Mamba SSM linear attention math olympiad PutnamBench Lean proof"` returned 10 results — none uses Mamba/linear-attention; PutnamBench/Seed-Prover/MA-LoT/InternLM-Math/STP are all dense-attention models. Confirms the asymmetric-decomposition gap.
**Why it matters for the spec.** The spec's third bullet (R / R+ / R++ asymmetric prediction) requires a benchmark suite that already separates retrieval from reasoning over the needle. NoCha + Loong do; current SubQ literature ignores them. Filling this cell is necessary before any "fusion is productive" claim becomes falsifiable.

### 7. The "compounding interaction" between Tunnel Vision (which cuts AGAINST naive recursion) and Lost-in-the-Middle / Hyper-multi-step (which plausibly favor it) has no benchmark that pulls both axes apart simultaneously, despite all three being load-bearing for the fusion thesis.

**Type:** Contradiction × untested-assumption.
**Statement.** Tunnel Vision / ParaThinker (2509.04475) documents that long sequential CoTs lock onto early wrong paths and *worsen* with depth — a failure mode that argues against naive K-pass recursion. Lost in the Middle (2307.03172) and Hyper-multi-step (2410.04422) document U-shaped position bias and super-linear multi-matching cost — failure modes recursion plausibly mitigates by re-reading. Score Dilution at Test Time (2512.13898) and Long Context, Less Focus (2602.15028) further document attention dilution. None of these papers has been replicated under either subquadratic attention (NSA/MoBA/DSA/Quest) or under TRM-style recursion, so the compound interaction is uncharacterized. Position-Bias-Emergence (2502.01951) gives a graph-theoretic argument that the same emergence holds under sparse masks — directly load-bearing for whether SubQ+recursion compose, but un-tested empirically. Falsifiable axis: on a position-controlled NoLiMa-style stimulus (without the NoLiMa license), measure (i) Tunnel-Vision lock-in rate, (ii) Lost-in-the-Middle U-curve depth, and (iii) Hyper-multi-step super-linearity exponent — each as a function of (sparse-attention pattern × architectural recursion K).
**Evidence:** Tunnel Vision / ParaThinker (2509.04475); Lost in the Middle (2307.03172); Hyper-multi-step (2410.04422); Score Dilution at Test Time (2512.13898); Long Context, Less Focus (2602.15028); Position Bias Emergence (2502.01951); NoLiMa (2502.05167, license-flagged).
**Verification query:** `hf_papers search "tunnel vision parallel thinking recursion failure mode"` returned 10 results — ParaThinker (2509.04475), Visual Para-Thinker (2602.13310), OPE (2602.08344), Parallel-R1 (2509.07980), Parallel-Probe (2602.03845), Thought Rollback (2412.19707), VL-Rethinker (2504.08837), MMaDA-Parallel (2511.09611), To-Backtrack-or-Not (2504.07052), Agentic RL Code Repair (2510.22075). Confirms the (architectural recursion × Tunnel Vision under SubQ) intersection is empty.
**Why it matters for the spec.** Tunnel Vision is the single strongest argument the spec's red-team will marshal against naive recursion-on-SubQ. A benchmark setup that triangulates Tunnel-Vision lock-in × Lost-in-the-Middle × Hyper-multi-step under (sparsity × K_arch) is the exact instrument needed to either falsify the fusion or constrain its valid regime — and it doesn't exist.

### 8. CRUXEval (input/output execution prediction) cleanly separates loop/recursion *depth in the program* from context length, but no architectural-recursion paper has ever evaluated whether forward-pass recursion K helps with simulating program-recursion depth in the *trace*.

**Type:** Untested assumption (looped-transformer literature universally assumes "K loops simulate K iterations of any algorithm" but never tests the program-execution case at fixed context).
**Statement.** CRUXEval (2401.03065) provides 800 Python functions where loop and recursion depth are explicit per-instance, with CoT-helpful and CoT-harmful sub-splits — i.e., a chain-length axis at fixed (low) context. Looped Transformers Are Better at Learning Learning Algorithms (2311.12424), On Expressive Power of Looped Transformers (2410.01405), Reasoning with Latent Thoughts (2502.17416), Hyperloop (2604.21254), and SCORE (2603.10544) all argue forward-pass loops simulate iterative algorithms in theory; none measures whether K_arch matches program-recursion-depth in CRUXEval traces. CRUXEval-X (2408.13001) extends to 19 languages but still only evaluates dense models. Transformer-Based Models Are Not Yet Perfect at Learning to Emulate Structural Recursion (2401.12947) gives the negative-result baseline for dense; no recursive-arch counterpart exists. Falsifiable axis: success-rate on CRUXEval as a function of (program-loop-depth × architectural-recursion-K), expecting a diagonal advantage when K_arch matches program loop depth that does not exist for K_arch=1.
**Evidence:** CRUXEval (2401.03065), CRUXEval-X (2408.13001); Transformer Structural Recursion (2401.12947); recursive architectures (2311.12424, 2410.01405, 2502.17416, 2604.21254, 2603.10544, 2510.04871, 2510.25741, 2602.11451).
**Verification query:** `hf_papers search "CRUXEval recursion depth output execution looped"` returned 10 results — CRUXEval, CRUXEval-X, Neural Debugger (2603.09951), CodeMind/REval (2501.18482), SpiralFormer (2602.11698), λ-RLM (2603.20105, novel and recent), Transformer Structural Recursion (2401.12947), RecursiveVLM (2602.09080), SemCoder (2406.01006), From-Growing-to-Looping (2602.16490). The only paper that pairs CRUXEval-style execution reasoning with looped/recursive transformers is Transformer-Based Models Are Not Yet Perfect (2401.12947) — and that's a *negative* result on dense; no recursive-arch CRUXEval evaluation exists.
**Why it matters for the spec.** CRUXEval is the cheapest possible falsification of the looped-transformer claim that K passes ≈ K iterations; the spec needs this measurement before extrapolating to long-context program synthesis on SWE-bench.

### 9. The retrieval-head structure that mechanistically explains long-context factuality has been studied under dense attention only — whether sparse-attention patterns and architectural recursion preserve, displace, or destroy retrieval-head structure has no published instrument despite the existence of a benchmark for the question.

**Type:** Unexplored intersection (retrieval-head structure × subquadratic attention × recursion depth).
**Statement.** Retrieval Head (2404.15574) identifies a small set of attention heads responsible for arbitrary-position fact retrieval and shows it explains hallucination patterns. RazorAttention (2407.15891) leverages retrieval heads for KV cache compression. Retrieval-Aware Distillation for Transformer-SSM Hybrids (2602.11374) explicitly preserves retrieval heads when distilling Transformers into SSMs — confirming retrieval-head structure is fragile under naive SSM conversion. Whether NSA/MoBA/DSA/Quest preserve retrieval-head structure (and whether architectural recursion can re-form it across passes if sparse attention destroys it on pass 1) is uncharacterized. NoLiMa (2502.05167, license-flagged) and Beyond the Needle's Illusion (2601.20276) provide clean instruments for measuring retrieval-head function under perturbation. Falsifiable axis: retrieval-head retention score (per 2404.15574 method) as a function of (sparse-attention pattern × K_arch); predict that retention drops monotonically in sparsity at K=1 but recovers with K>1 if recursion re-allocates heads.
**Evidence:** Retrieval Head (2404.15574); RazorAttention (2407.15891); Retrieval-Aware Distillation for Transformer-SSM Hybrids (2602.11374); subquadratic-attention NSA cluster (NSA 2502.11089, MoBA 2502.13189, DSA 2512.02556, Quest); NoLiMa (2502.05167); Beyond the Needle's Illusion (2601.20276); architectural recursion: see gap 1.
**Verification query:** `hf_papers search "retrieval head sparse attention preserve destroy NIAH"` returned 10 results: Retrieval Head (2404.15574), Retrieval-Aware Distillation for Transformer-SSM Hybrids (2602.11374), Interleaved Head Attention (2602.21371), RazorAttention (2407.15891), SparseD (2509.24014), DHA (2406.06567), NOSA (2510.13602), Beyond the Needle's Illusion (2601.20276), Knocking-Heads Attention (2510.23052), Hydra (2603.28554). No paper measures retrieval-head structure under (sparse-attention pattern × recursion-depth).
**Why it matters for the spec.** If sparse attention destroys retrieval-head structure and recursion cannot re-form it, the fusion is incoherent on R-style long-context tasks — a clean falsification path. If recursion does re-form it, that is the strongest possible positive evidence for the fusion thesis.

## Discarded candidate gaps

### Discarded 1. "No subquadratic-attention model has been evaluated on RULER."

**Why considered.** Scout-4 lists RULER (2404.06654) as the canonical multi-task long-context benchmark; scout-3 cluster on Mamba/SSM long-context capability seemed plausibly absent from RULER.
**Verification query.** `hf_papers search "Mamba RULER NIAH long context evaluation hybrid"` returned 10 results, including LongMamba (2504.16053), ReMamba (2408.15496), Mamba-2 hybrid (2406.07887), and Overflow Prevention Enhances Long-Context Recurrent LLMs (2505.07793, which targets LongBench v2 with subquadratic recurrent models). RULER and LongBench have been measured against SSMs in several places.
**Discarded because.** The bare "SubQ × RULER" intersection is in fact populated. The remaining gap is not at the SubQ × benchmark seam but at the (SubQ × architectural recursion × benchmark) seam — captured in gaps 1 and 4 above. Claiming a flat "RULER × SubQ" gap would have been false.

### Discarded 2. "No recursive transformer has been evaluated on long-context program synthesis (LongCodeBench / Long Code Arena)."

**Why considered.** Scout-4 cites Long Code Arena (2406.11612) and scout-5 references LongCodeBench (2505.07897); scout-1's recursive-transformer cluster is consistently short-context.
**Verification query.** `hf_papers search "recursion depth axis fixed context length benchmark"` (10 results) returned LongCodeBench (2505.07897) and LoCoBench (2509.09614) but no recursive-transformer evaluation on either; however, `hf_papers search "recursive transformer SWE-bench code repository"` (10 results) returned λ-RLM / Y-Combinator-for-LLMs (2603.20105), which explicitly uses recursive language models for long-context tasks and reports gains on long-context-rot benchmarks.
**Discarded because.** λ-RLM (2603.20105) — although it operates at the program-runtime layer, not architectural recursion in the forward pass — does cover the (recursion × long-context code) intersection well enough that the bare gap claim "nobody has tried recursion on long-context code" would be misleading. The remaining sharper gap is captured in gap 6: SWE-bench-Verified/Pro stratified by edit-LOC, *architectural* (not runtime) recursion, K-axis. Care must be taken in hypothesis-smith stage to distinguish these.

### Discarded 3. "Frontier benchmarks like FrontierMath have not been evaluated on subquadratic-attention models."

**Why considered.** Scout-5 flags FrontierMath (2411.04872) as gated, with closed scoring.
**Verification query.** `hf_papers search "Mamba SSM linear attention math olympiad PutnamBench Lean proof"` returned 10 results; no SSM is evaluated on FrontierMath in the open literature.
**Discarded because.** While the intersection is empty, FrontierMath is a *closed* gated benchmark (per scout-5). The gap is non-actionable: any claim "no SubQ on FrontierMath" cannot be remediated by an open swarm because the benchmark is gated. This belongs in a license-flag list, not in an actionable gap. The actionable formulation is captured in gap 5 (PutnamBench/miniF2F/CoqGym × SSM × recursion) where the benchmarks are open.
